# Wichita Smart Homes - Market Entry Strategy

## Executive Summary

This go-to-market plan outlines a focused strategy to establish Wichita Smart Homes as the premier smart home partner for production home builders in the Wichita metropolitan area. By targeting the underserved builder segment with turnkey packages, competitive pricing, and sales enablement support, we can capture 15-20% of the new construction smart home market within 18 months.

**Target**: 150-200 home installations in Year 1, scaling to 400+ in Year 2

## Target Segments

### Primary: Production Home Builders

**Definition**: Builders constructing 50+ homes annually with standardized floor plans

**Wichita Market Profile**:
| Builder Tier | Annual Homes | Count | Total Homes |
|--------------|--------------|-------|-------------|
| Large (200+ homes) | 200-400 | 3 | 800 |
| Medium (100-199 homes) | 100-199 | 5 | 600 |
| Small Production (50-99) | 50-99 | 8 | 500 |
| **Total Production** | | **16** | **1,900** |

**Target Builders (Priority)**:
1. Builders with active communities in growing corridors (East/Northeast)
2. Builders targeting first-time and move-up buyers ($250K-$450K)
3. Builders without existing smart home partnerships
4. Builders with strong model home programs

### Secondary: Semi-Custom Builders

**Definition**: Builders doing 15-49 homes annually with some customization

- Lower priority due to complexity and volume
- Pursue opportunistically after production partnerships established
- Adapt packages for higher customization tolerance

### Not Targeting (Initially)

- Custom builders (too small, too complex)
- Multi-family developers (different product set)
- Remodelers/renovators (retrofit economics differ)

## Sales & Marketing Strategy

### Builder Acquisition

**Outreach Sequence**:
1. **Week 1**: LinkedIn connection + personalized InMail
2. **Week 2**: Email with market data and value proposition
3. **Week 3**: Phone call to schedule meeting
4. **Week 4**: In-person presentation with pitch deck
5. **Week 5**: Model home proposal and partnership terms
6. **Week 6+**: Follow-up and negotiation

**Key Messages**:
- "Your buyers want smart homes – we make it easy and profitable"
- "No upfront cost, no inventory risk, no training hassle"
- "Installed price competitive with DIY – but fully supported"
- "Differentiate your homes without adding complexity"

### Model Home Strategy

**Why Model Homes Matter**:
- Tangible demonstration of smart home experience
- Sales tool for builder reps
- Validation of partnership commitment
- Reference installation for production homes

**Model Home Approach**:
1. Install Premium package in first model home (at cost)
2. Create "smart home experience tour" script for sales reps
3. Develop leave-behind materials for buyers
4. Monthly refresh of demo content and scenarios

### Builder Sales Enablement

**Training Program**:
| Session | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Overview | 1 hour | Smart home market, buyer personas, our packages |
| Demo | 1 hour | Hands-on experience with all features |
| Selling | 2 hours | Objection handling, upgrade conversations, ROI |
| Process | 1 hour | Order flow, timelines, customer handoff |

**Support Materials**:
- Package comparison sheets (print + digital)
- ROI calculator for buyers

## Risk Assessment

### Key Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Builder partnership delays | Medium | High | Parallel outreach to 10+ builders |
| Supply chain disruption | Medium | Medium | Multi-vendor strategy, inventory buffer |
| Technician turnover | Medium | Medium | Competitive pay, training investment |
| Competitor response | Low | Medium | First-mover advantage, relationship lock-in |
| Economic downturn | Low | High | Focus on entry-level builders, reduce fixed costs |
| Technology shifts | Low | Low | Platform-agnostic approach (Matter) |

### Contingency Plans

**If builder partnerships lag**:
- Pivot to electrician referral model
- Consider limited direct-to-consumer pilot
- Reduce fixed costs, extend runway

**If installation quality issues arise**:
- Pause growth, focus on process improvement
- Hire experienced lead technician
- Implement more rigorous QC
